What is a country? What is a nationality?
A country is the place — like Brazil, the USA, or Colombia. A nationality is the adjective that describes a person from that place — Brazilian, American, Colombian. We use the verb to be with nationalities.
Country
She is from + country
She is from Brazil.
Nationality
She is + nationality
She is Brazilian.
✅ Affirmative (positive)
Affirmative
He is American.
Bruno Mars — born in Hawaii, USA
Affirmative
She is Brazilian.
Anitta — born in Rio de Janeiro
Affirmative
He is Puerto Rican.
Bad Bunny — born in Puerto Rico
Affirmative
She is Colombian.
Shakira — born in Barranquilla
❌ Negative — He / She is NOT...
Add not after the verb to be, or use the contraction isn't.
Negative — full form
Bruno Mars is not Brazilian.
He is American — from Hawaii!
Negative — contraction
Neymar isn't Spanish.
He is Brazilian — from São Paulo!
Negative — full form
Shakira is not from Brazil.
She is from Colombia.
Negative — contraction
Taylor Swift isn't British.
She is American — from Pennsylvania!
❓ Interrogative — questions
Put the verb to be before the subject for yes/no questions. Use Where or What nationality for open questions.
Yes / No question
Is Bad Bunny Colombian?
No, he isn't. He is Puerto Rican.
Yes / No question
Is Anitta Brazilian?
Yes, she is!
Where question
Where is Karol G from?
She is from Colombia.
Where question
Where is Ronaldo from?
He is from Portugal.
What nationality
What nationality is BTS?
They are South Korean.
What nationality
What nationality is Luan Santana?
He is Brazilian.
Quick Tips
1
Nationality adjectives are ALWAYS capitalized in English! Write Brazilian, not brazilian. Same for American, Colombian, Portuguese...
2
Use "is" for one person, "are" for a group. "Anitta is Brazilian." but "BTS are South Korean."
3
"Isn't" = is not. They mean exactly the same thing! Use contractions in conversation, full form in formal writing.
4
Questions: flip the subject and verb! "Anitta IS Brazilian." becomes "IS Anitta Brazilian?" — just move "is" to the front.
5
"Where are you from?" vs "What is your nationality?" Both correct! First is more conversational, second is more formal.
6
Short answers: "Is Taylor Swift American?" → "Yes, she is." or "No, she isn't." Always include the pronoun — just "Yes" sounds rude in English!
7
Never mix "from" with a nationality adjective. Say "She is FROM Brazil" OR "She is BRAZILIAN" — never "She is from Brazilian."
Patterns at a glance
Affirmative
Subject + is/are + nationality
She is Colombian.
Negative (full)
Subject + is/are + not + nationality
He is not British.
Negative (short)
Subject + isn't/aren't + nationality
He isn't British.
Yes/No question
Is/Are + subject + nationality?
Is she Korean?
Where question
Where + is/are + subject + from?
Where is he from?
What nationality
What nationality + is/are + subject?
What nationality is she?
