Country vs Nationality
A country is the place — Brazil, USA, Colombia. A nationality is the adjective — Brazilian, American, Colombian. We use to be with nationalities.
Country
She is from + country
She is from Brazil.
Nationality
She is + nationality
She is Brazilian.
✅ Affirmative
Affirmative
He is American.
Bruno Mars — born in Hawaii, USA
Affirmative
She is Brazilian.
Anitta — born in Rio de Janeiro
Affirmative
He is Puerto Rican.
Bad Bunny — born in Puerto Rico
Affirmative
She is Colombian.
Shakira — born in Barranquilla
❌ Negative
Add not after to be, or use the contraction isn't.
Full form
Bruno Mars is not Brazilian.
He is American — from Hawaii!
Contraction
Neymar isn't Spanish.
He is Brazilian — from São Paulo!
Full form
Shakira is not from Brazil.
She is from Colombia.
Contraction
Taylor Swift isn't British.
She is American — from Pennsylvania!
❓ Interrogative
Put to be before the subject. Use Where or What nationality for open questions.
Yes / No
Is Bad Bunny Colombian?
No, he isn't. He is Puerto Rican.
Yes / No
Is Anitta Brazilian?
Yes, she is!
Where question
Where is Karol G from?
She is from Colombia.
Where question
Where is Ronaldo from?
He is from Portugal.
What nationality
What nationality is BTS?
They are South Korean.
What nationality
What nationality is Luan Santana?
He is Brazilian.
Quick Tips
1
Nationality adjectives are ALWAYS capitalized! Write Brazilian, not brazilian.
2
Use "is" for one person, "are" for a group. "Anitta is Brazilian." / "BTS are South Korean."
3
"Isn't" = is not. Same meaning! Use contractions in speaking, full form in writing.
4
Questions: flip subject and verb! "Anitta IS Brazilian." → "IS Anitta Brazilian?"
5
"Where are you from?" vs "What is your nationality?" Both correct! First is casual, second is formal.
6
Short answers need the pronoun: "Yes, she is." / "No, she isn't." — Just "Yes" alone sounds rude!
7
Never mix "from" + nationality adjective. Say "She is FROM Brazil" OR "She is BRAZILIAN" — never "She is from Brazilian."
Patterns at a glance
Affirmative
Subject + is/are + nationality
She is Colombian.
Negative (full)
Subject + is/are + not + nat.
He is not British.
Negative (short)
Subject + isn't/aren't + nat.
He isn't British.
Yes/No question
Is/Are + subject + nat?
Is she Korean?
Where question
Where + is/are + subject + from?
Where is he from?
What nationality
What nationality + is/are + subj?
What nationality is she?
